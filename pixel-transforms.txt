PIXEL TRANSFORMS
================

--- PIXEL BURST ---
Each pixel flies outward from a configurable focal point, then returns home.
The first half of the animation scatters pixels away using radial direction vectors;
the second half pulls them back to their destination positions.
A spin option blends the radial direction with a clockwise or counter-clockwise tangent,
giving the explosion a swirling quality.
Spread controls how far pixels travel, Speed controls duration, and Easing (Smooth/Sharp/Snappy)
shapes the acceleration curve. Hold delays the next frame after pixels settle.
Optional focal point X/Y sliders let you bias the explosion origin anywhere in the canvas.

--- PIXEL POP ---
A two-phase explode/unsplode effect with per-pixel randomized speeds and gravity.
Explode phase: pixels scatter outward with individual speed variance (Gravity Push controls spread),
while a separate gravity target (Gravity Pull + focal X/Y) bends all trajectories toward a point.
Extend Duration continues outward travel past the explode peak using the same physics (per-pixel speeds,
gravity pull, easing) — the radial push stays frozen at peak while gravity keeps accumulating, so the
extend phase feels like a seamless continuation of the explode rather than a new linear drift.
A configurable Delay holds pixels at their furthest position before the snap-back begins.
Unsplode phase: pixels return to their destination with a snappy two-stage pull — slow glide until
the Unsplode Strength threshold, then a rapid magnetic snap to the final position.
Stay in Canvas keeps pixels inside the canvas boundary. Wall Bounce % (0–100) controls how much
velocity is retained on each wall hit — 0 absorbs all energy (soft stop), 100 is elastic (full bounce),
default 50. Pixels can bounce up to 3 times per frame before a hard clamp.
Stay at Focus toggles a black-hole attraction toward the Gravity Target focal point. The pull force
scales with proximity squared (much stronger near the center) and uses Gravity Pull as its base
strength, so it competes with the outward explode force throughout the entire animation — creating
orbit, spiral, or condensed-scatter effects depending on relative strengths.

--- PIXEL MORPH ---
Smoothly transitions pixel-by-pixel from one frame to the next using three categories:
Stable pixels (same color in both frames) render immediately at full opacity.
Birth pixels (new in destination frame) slide in from nearby stable neighbors in a wave that
ripples outward from the stable region — closer births arrive earlier.
Death pixels (present in source frame but gone in destination) shrink centered from full cell size
to nothing over the animation duration. With Fade Dying Pixels enabled, dying pixels also fade
their alpha from fully opaque to transparent simultaneously with the shrink, creating a combined
dissolve+shrink effect. Without it, pixels shrink sharply with hard edges.
Speed (ms) and Hold (ms) control animation duration and the pause between transitions.
The Focal Point X/Y sliders bias where the birth wave originates.

--- PIXEL TWIST ---
Each pixel rotates in place around its center, clockwise or counter-clockwise, across two independent half-turns.
The first half covers 0→180°; the second half either continues to 360° (full spin) or reverses to 0° (half spin).
When multiple animation frames are loaded, the first frame renders during the first half and the second frame during the second half.
Speed sliders independently set the duration of each half — 0 ms is an instant snap, 100 ms is the minimum, up to 1000 ms (1 second) at maximum.
Smooth spin sliders (one per half) blend between uniform rotation (0) and a sine ease-in/ease-out curve (100), where speed
ramps up from rest, peaks at full speed through the middle of the phase, and decelerates symmetrically back to a stop.
A Spread checkbox expands a small gap between adjacent pixels so the rotation plane is visually distinct rather than a flat color flip.

--- PIXEL SPRING (JELLY) ---
Each pixel is tethered to its home cell by a damped spring and settles into place with a
gelatinous overshoot/wobble. Unlike the other transforms, motion is not a closed-form curve —
it is integrated frame-by-frame (semi-implicit Euler) by the shared PixelIntegrator using a
damped-harmonic-oscillator acceleration: accel = -k*(pos - home) - c*velocity.
Pixels start at the source-frame position with an outward radial impulse (from the Burst focal
point) and spring toward home. When the source and destination frames have the same pixel count,
home is the destination shape (so the sprite springs into the next frame); otherwise pixels
wobble back to their own positions in place.
Stiffness sets the spring constant k (higher = faster, tighter oscillation).
Damping is expressed as a percentage of critical damping: 100% settles smoothly with almost no
overshoot, lower values are bouncier/jellier, and 0% oscillates freely until the duration ends.
Impulse sets the initial outward kick (0 = pure spring from the source displacement).
Duration (ms) is how long the effect runs before the next frame; Hold (ms) pauses after it.

--- EASING STYLES ---
The shared easing library (used by Pixel Burst's Easing dropdown, among others) offers:
Smooth (quadratic), Sharp (linear), Snappy (quartic), Elastic (spring-like overshoot with
oscillation), Bounce (ball-drop settle), and Overshoot (single back/overshoot past the target).
