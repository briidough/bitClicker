PIXEL TRANSFORMS
================

--- PIXEL BURST ---
Each pixel flies outward from a configurable focal point, then returns home.
The first half of the animation scatters pixels away using radial direction vectors;
the second half pulls them back to their destination positions.
A spin option blends the radial direction with a clockwise or counter-clockwise tangent,
giving the explosion a swirling quality.
Spread controls how far pixels travel, Speed controls duration, and Easing (Smooth/Sharp/Snappy)
shapes the acceleration curve. Hold delays the next frame after pixels settle.
Optional focal point X/Y sliders let you bias the explosion origin anywhere in the canvas.

--- PIXEL POP ---
A two-phase explode/unsplode effect with per-pixel randomized speeds and gravity.
Explode phase: pixels scatter outward with individual speed variance (Gravity Push controls spread),
while a separate gravity target (Gravity Pull + focal X/Y) bends all trajectories toward a point.
An optional Extend phase continues the outward travel past the explode peak, held in place for
Extend Duration ms before release.
A configurable Delay holds pixels at their furthest position before the snap-back begins.
Unsplode phase: pixels return to their destination with a snappy two-stage pull — slow glide until
the Unsplode Strength threshold, then a rapid magnetic snap to the final position.
Stay in Canvas clamps all pixels to the canvas boundary during flight.

--- PIXEL TWIST ---
Each pixel rotates in place around its center, clockwise or counter-clockwise, across two independent half-turns.
The first half covers 0→180°; the second half either continues to 360° (full spin) or reverses to 0° (half spin).
When multiple animation frames are loaded, the first frame renders during the first half and the second frame during the second half.
Speed sliders independently set the duration of each half — 0 ms is an instant snap, 100 ms is the minimum, up to 1000 ms (1 second) at maximum.
Smooth spin sliders (one per half) blend between uniform rotation (0) and a sine ease-in/ease-out curve (100), where speed
ramps up from rest, peaks at full speed through the middle of the phase, and decelerates symmetrically back to a stop.
A Spread checkbox expands a small gap between adjacent pixels so the rotation plane is visually distinct rather than a flat color flip.
